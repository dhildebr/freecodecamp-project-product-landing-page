I'm an adventurer, which means the good people of Gielinor point me towards someone who deserves it and I leave a trail of corpses between here and there. To earn any money in this trade, you learn to strip everything you kill of anything sufficiently light or valuable, to sell when you get back to town. With the partitioned bag of holding, I can haul about the half dozen swords and axes I inexlicably found in a dragon's belly and not worry about losing that enchanted ring I found among the clutter.

I'm what you could call a power-user. Before the partitioned bag, everything went in one or a handful of sack and pouches. My runestones tumbled between my scrolls, my scrolls went crashing into my potions, my potions spilled all over my journals – and a foul black slurry pooled beneath it all. Now everything is segregated into one of potentially numberous demiplanes. This sets a new standard for pocket dimension storage solutions!
